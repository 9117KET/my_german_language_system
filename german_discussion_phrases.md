# German Discussion, Agreement, Proposal & Discourse Phrases

> 80/20 Guide: `[CORE]` = high-frequency, handles ~80% of real situations. `[EXTENDED]` = useful but situational.
> Phrases already covered in the islands (daily life, greetings, core structures) are NOT repeated here.

---

## 1. Modal Particles (Modalpartikeln)

> These are uniquely German "flavor words" - they do not have direct English translations but completely change the tone, attitude, and feeling of a sentence. They cannot be skipped in natural German.

---

### `ja` [CORE]
**Rule:** Signals that something is shared knowledge, mildly obvious, or surprisingly true. Adds a "you know" or "isn't it" feeling. Never used to mean "yes" in this role.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Das ist ja interessant. | That's actually interesting. / Interesting, isn't it. |
| Du weißt ja, dass... | You know that... |
| Das kann ja nicht sein! | That just can't be! |
| Das ist ja das Problem. | That's exactly the problem, you know. |
| Er ist ja noch jung. | He is still young, after all. |

**Example:** "Das ist ja eine gute Idee!" - That's actually a great idea! (tone: pleasantly surprised)

---

### `doch` [CORE]
**Rule:** Contradicts a negative assumption, adds emphasis to a statement, or softens a command. One of the most versatile particles. Can mean "but", "after all", "come on", "surely", or "do/does".

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Das stimmt doch. | But that IS true. / That IS correct. |
| Komm doch mit! | Come along, won't you! / Just come along! |
| Das ist doch klar. | That's obvious, surely. |
| Du kannst das doch! | You CAN do that! |
| Das haben wir doch besprochen. | But we did discuss that. |
| Mach dir doch keine Sorgen. | Come on, don't worry. |

**Example:** "Das wusstest du doch!" - But you knew that! (contradicting a denial)

---

### `mal` [CORE]
**Rule:** Softens a request or command, making it casual and friendly instead of demanding. Like "just" or "for a moment" in English. Always makes the tone lighter.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Hör mal! | Listen! / Hey, listen! |
| Schau mal. | Have a look. / Look at this. |
| Warte mal kurz. | Just wait a moment. |
| Versuch es mal. | Just give it a try. |
| Denk mal nach. | Just think about it. |
| Komm mal her. | Come here for a second. |

**Example:** "Kannst du mal kurz helfen?" - Can you just help for a moment? (sounds casual, not demanding)

---

### `denn` [CORE]
**Rule:** Used in questions to express genuine curiosity, mild impatience, or surprise. Like "so", "then", or "on earth" in English questions. Makes the question feel more natural and engaged.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Was ist denn los? | So what's going on? / What on earth is happening? |
| Warum denn nicht? | But why not? / So why not? |
| Wie heißt du denn? | So what's your name then? |
| Was meinst du denn damit? | So what do you mean by that? |
| Wo warst du denn? | So where were you then? |
| Was soll das denn? | What's that supposed to mean? |

**Example:** "Was machst du denn hier?" - So what are you doing here? (tone: surprised but curious)

---

### `eben` / `halt` [CORE]
**Rule:** Signals that something is simply the way it is - unavoidable, obvious, or a matter of fact. "eben" is more standard/northern German, "halt" is more colloquial/southern/Bavarian. Like "just" or "that's just how it is".

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Das ist eben so. | That's just the way it is. |
| So läuft das halt. | That's just how it goes. |
| Das muss eben sein. | It just has to be that way. |
| Man kann halt nicht alles haben. | You just can't have everything. |
| Das ist eben das Problem. | That's simply the problem. |
| So ist das Leben halt. | That's just life. |

**Example:** "Das ist halt das Leben." - That's just life. (accepting, resigned tone)

---

### `schon` [CORE]
**Rule:** Adds certainty or reassurance ("it will work out"), OR signals a concession ("yes, but..."). Different from "schon" meaning "already" - here it adds emotional weight.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Das wird schon klappen. | It'll work out, you'll see. |
| Ja, schon, aber... | Yes, sure, but... (conceding before a contrast) |
| Das kenne ich schon. | I know that already / I'm aware. |
| Das schaffen wir schon! | We'll manage that, don't worry! |
| Das stimmt schon. | That's true enough. |
| Es wird schon gut gehen. | It'll be fine, trust me. |

**Example:** "Das schaffen wir schon!" - We'll manage it! (tone: confident, reassuring)

---

### `wohl` [EXTENDED]
**Rule:** Expresses probability, polite assumption, or uncertainty. Like "probably", "I suppose", or "I would think". Signals you're not 100% sure.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Das ist wohl so. | That's probably the case. |
| Er ist wohl krank. | He's probably sick. |
| Das dürfte wohl stimmen. | That's probably correct. |
| Das wird wohl nicht einfach sein. | That probably won't be easy. |
| Das hat er wohl vergessen. | He's probably forgotten that. |

**Example:** "Das wird wohl seine Meinung sein." - That's probably his opinion.

---

### `eigentlich` [CORE]
**Rule:** As a particle (not an adverb), it means "actually", "to be honest", or "come to think of it". Often signals a contrast, a redirect, or a correction of an assumption.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Eigentlich wollte ich... | I actually wanted to... |
| Was wollte ich eigentlich sagen? | What was I actually going to say? |
| Ich bin eigentlich dagegen. | I'm actually against it. |
| Das ist eigentlich gar nicht schlecht. | That's actually not bad at all. |
| Eigentlich habe ich keine Zeit. | I actually don't have time. |

**Example:** "Eigentlich habe ich keine Zeit, aber okay." - I actually don't have time, but okay. (reluctant agreement)

---

### `bloß` / `nur` [EXTENDED]
**Rule:** Adds urgency, warning, or strict restriction. "bloß" is more emotionally charged (warnings, threats, wishes); "nur" is more neutral (just, only). Both translate as "just" or "only" but with different emotional weight.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Mach das bloß nicht! | Don't you dare do that! |
| Wenn das bloß klappt... | If only that would work... |
| Ich will nur kurz fragen. | I just want to ask quickly. |
| Sag das bloß nicht zu ihm! | Whatever you do, don't say that to him! |
| Nur keine Panik. | Just don't panic. |
| Das weiß ich bloß nicht. | I just don't know that. |

**Example:** "Sag das bloß nicht laut!" - Don't say that out loud, whatever you do!

---

### `ruhig` [EXTENDED]
**Rule:** Gives relaxed permission or gentle encouragement. Like "go ahead", "feel free", "by all means". Removes any hesitation or awkwardness.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Das kannst du ruhig sagen. | You can go ahead and say that. |
| Geh ruhig schon vor. | Go on ahead, it's fine. |
| Frag ruhig. | Feel free to ask. |
| Du kannst ruhig ehrlich sein. | You can be honest, it's okay. |
| Nimm dir ruhig mehr Zeit. | Take all the time you need. |

**Example:** "Du kannst das ruhig machen." - Feel free to do that. / Go right ahead.

---

### `etwa` [EXTENDED]
**Rule:** In questions, expresses disbelief or shocked surprise ("surely not?", "don't tell me..."). As a standalone adverb it means "approximately" - but the particle use is what catches learners off guard.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Bist du etwa krank? | Don't tell me you're sick? |
| Hast du das etwa vergessen? | Surely you didn't forget that? |
| Das kann doch nicht etwa wahr sein. | That surely can't be true. |
| Meinst du das etwa ernst? | Do you actually mean that seriously? |

**Example:** "Hast du das etwa nicht gewusst?" - Don't tell me you didn't know that?

---

### `zwar` [CORE]
**Rule:** Concedes a point before introducing a contrast. Always paired with "aber" or "jedoch". Like "admittedly", "granted", or "it's true that... but". The contrast after "aber" is the real point.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Das ist zwar richtig, aber... | That's true enough, but... |
| Ich komme zwar gerne, aber... | I'd love to come, but... |
| Zwar verstehe ich das, aber... | I do understand that, but... |
| Das ist zwar teuer, aber es lohnt sich. | It's admittedly expensive, but it's worth it. |
| Ich kenne ihn zwar, aber nicht gut. | I do know him, but not well. |

**Example:** "Das stimmt zwar, aber das ändert nichts." - That may be true, but it changes nothing.

## 2. Agreement (Zustimmung)

---

### Full Agreement [CORE]

| German | English | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Genau! | Exactly! | Most common, works in almost any context |
| Stimmt! | That's right! / True! | Very common, casual |
| Ja, genau. | Yes, exactly. | Softer than "Genau!" alone |
| Da hast du recht. | You're right about that. | Personal, acknowledges the other person |
| Das sehe ich genauso. | I see it the same way. | Good in discussions |
| Einverstanden. | Agreed. | More formal, finalizes a decision |
| Absolut. | Absolutely. | Strong agreement |
| Klar! | Of course! / Sure! | Casual, enthusiastic |

**Example:** "Sollen wir morgen treffen?" - "Ja, klar! / Einverstanden."

---

### Partial / Thoughtful Agreement [EXTENDED]

| German | English | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Das finde ich auch. | I think so too. | |
| Völlig richtig. | Absolutely right. | Slightly formal |
| Ganz meiner Meinung. | Totally my opinion. | |
| Ich stimme (dir/Ihnen) zu. | I agree (with you). | "dir" casual, "Ihnen" formal |
| Da bin ich (voll) dabei. | I'm (totally) on board. | Colloquial |
| Da hast du einen Punkt. | You have a point there. | Acknowledges a valid argument |
| Das kann ich nur bestätigen. | I can only confirm that. | Formal |
| Ja, das macht Sinn. | Yes, that makes sense. | Very common in discussions |
| Na klar! | Of course! | More emphatic than "klar" |
| Das sehe ich ähnlich. | I see it similarly. | Soft agreement with slight room for difference |

## 3. Disagreement & Pushback (Widerspruch)

> Tip: Germans generally appreciate direct but polite disagreement. Hedging with "eigentlich" or "irgendwie" softens the pushback considerably.

---

### Direct Disagreement [CORE]

| German | English | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Das sehe ich anders. | I see it differently. | Neutral, non-aggressive |
| Ich bin anderer Meinung. | I have a different opinion. | Formal-ish |
| Da widerspreche ich (dir/Ihnen). | I disagree with that. | Direct |
| Das stimmt nicht ganz. | That's not entirely correct. | Softened - leaves room |
| Nein, das glaube ich nicht. | No, I don't think so. | Straightforward |

**Example:** "Das sehe ich anders - ich denke, wir sollten warten."

---

### Softer Pushback [EXTENDED]

| German | English | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Das bezweifle ich. | I doubt that. | |
| Ich bin da nicht so sicher. | I'm not so sure about that. | Gentle, leaves door open |
| Moment mal, das sehe ich anders. | Wait a moment, I see that differently. | Interrupting politely |
| Das mag sein, aber... | That may be, but... | Concedes before countering |
| Das halte ich für falsch. | I think that's wrong. | Stronger |
| Ich bin (damit) nicht einverstanden. | I don't agree with that. | Formal |
| Das ist nicht ganz richtig. | That's not quite right. | Gentle correction |
| Ich sehe das etwas anders. | I see that a little differently. | Very soft |
| Da muss ich widersprechen. | I have to disagree there. | Signals you feel strongly |
| Das würde ich so nicht sagen. | I wouldn't put it that way. | Diplomatic |

## 4. Discussion Connectors (Gesprächsverbinder)

> These are the "glue" of any structured discussion - they signal how your next point relates to the last.

---

### Opening & Sequencing [CORE]

| German | English |
|---|---|
| Zunächst einmal... | First of all... |
| Erstens... zweitens... | First... second... |
| Vor allem... | Above all / Most importantly... |
| Als erstes möchte ich sagen... | First I'd like to say... |
| Ich fange damit an, dass... | I'll start with the fact that... |

---

### Adding & Expanding [CORE]

| German | English |
|---|---|
| Außerdem... | Besides / Furthermore... |
| Dazu kommt noch... | On top of that... |
| Darüber hinaus... | Beyond that / Moreover... |
| Und auch... | And also... |
| Nicht nur das, sondern auch... | Not only that, but also... |

---

### Contrasting [CORE]

| German | English |
|---|---|

| Allerdings... | However / That said... |
| Im Gegensatz dazu... | In contrast to that... |
| Obwohl... | Although... |
| Dennoch... | Yet / Nevertheless... |

---

### Reasoning & Concluding [CORE]

| German | English |
|---|---|
| Deshalb... / Deswegen... | Therefore / That's why... |
| Das heißt,... | That means... / In other words... |
| Um das zusammenzufassen... | To summarize... |
| Zum Beispiel... | For example... |
| Im Allgemeinen... | In general... |
| Abgesehen davon... | Apart from that... |

---

### Redirecting & Clarifying [EXTENDED]

| German | English |
|---|---|

## 5. Making Proposals (Vorschläge machen)

---

### Casual Proposals [CORE]

| German | English |
|---|---|
| Wie wäre es mit...? | How about...? |
| Was wäre, wenn...? | What if...? |
| Vielleicht sollten wir... | Maybe we should... |
| Man könnte... | One could... / We could... |
| Wie wäre es, wenn wir...? | What if we...? |

**Example:** "Wie wäre es mit einem Kaffee zuerst?" - How about a coffee first?

---

### Formal Proposals [EXTENDED]

| German | English |
|---|---|
| Ich schlage vor, dass... | I suggest that... |
| Ich würde vorschlagen,... | I would suggest... |
| Darf ich einen Vorschlag machen? | May I make a suggestion? |
| Ich hätte da eine Idee. | I have an idea. |
| Wäre es nicht besser, wenn...? | Wouldn't it be better if...? |
| Es wäre sinnvoll,... | It would make sense to... |
| Könnten wir nicht...? | Couldn't we...? |
| Was hältst du davon, wenn...? | What do you think about (doing)...? |
| Was würdest du davon halten, wenn...? | What would you think if...? |

## 6. Hedging & Softening (Abschwächen)

> Use these to sound more natural, less absolute, and more nuanced - exactly how native speakers talk.

---

### [CORE]

| German | English | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Eigentlich... | Actually... | Introduces a contrast or correction |
| Irgendwie... | Somehow... | Vague connection or feeling |
| Im Großen und Ganzen... | By and large / Overall... | General summary |
| Mehr oder weniger... | More or less... | Approximate truth |
| Im Prinzip... | In principle... | True in theory, maybe not in practice |
| Ich würde sagen,... | I would say... | Softens a strong statement |
| Sozusagen... | So to speak... | Acknowledges the word/phrase is approximate |

**Example:** "Ich würde sagen, das ist mehr oder weniger richtig." - I would say that's more or less correct.

---

### [EXTENDED]

| German | English |
|---|---|
| Gewissermaßen... | To some extent / In a way... |
| In gewissem Sinne... | In a certain sense... |
| Theoretisch... | Theoretically... |
| Wenn man so will... | If you will... / In a way... |
| Das könnte man so sehen. | One could see it that way. |
| Es kommt darauf an. | It depends. |
| Das ist schwer zu sagen. | That's hard to say. |
| Nicht unbedingt. | Not necessarily. |

## 7. Scope & Quantity One-Worders

> Format: **Word** - Rule - Common Phrases - Example

---

### `sogar` (even) [CORE]
**Rule:** Adds emphasis by including something unexpected or surprising. The thing marked by "sogar" is at the extreme end of what you'd expect.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Das ist sogar besser als erwartet. | That's even better than expected. |
| Sogar er hat mitgemacht. | Even he joined in. |
| Das hat sogar mein Chef gelobt. | Even my boss praised that. |
| Sogar ich kann das verstehen. | Even I can understand that. |
| Das macht es sogar schwieriger. | That makes it even harder. |

**Example:** "Das war sogar einfacher als ich dachte." - That was even easier than I thought.

---

### `alle` (everyone / all) [CORE]
**Rule:** Refers to a group as a whole. Use "alle" for people and things as a group. Not interchangeable with "jeder" (each individual).

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Alle wissen das. | Everyone knows that. |
| Alle haben zugestimmt. | Everyone agreed. |
| Das gilt für alle. | That applies to everyone. |
| Alle zusammen | All together |
| Vor allem anderen... | Above everything else... |
| Wir alle | All of us |

**Example:** "Alle haben zugestimmt, also machen wir es so." - Everyone agreed, so we'll do it that way.

---

### `jeder` (everyone / each person) [CORE]
**Rule:** Refers to each individual one by one, not as a group. "Jeder kann..." = "Any person is capable of..."

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Jeder kann das lernen. | Anyone can learn that. |
| Jeder für sich. | Everyone for themselves. |
| Jeder hat seine Meinung. | Everyone has their opinion. |
| Nicht jeder denkt so. | Not everyone thinks that way. |
| So gut wie jeder | Just about everyone |

**Example:** "Jeder macht Fehler." - Everyone makes mistakes. (each person individually)

---

### `alles` (everything) [CORE]
**Rule:** Refers to the totality of things. Very common in set expressions.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Alles klar! | All good! / Got it! |
| Alles in Ordnung. | Everything's fine. |
| Alles oder nichts. | All or nothing. |
| Alles Gute! | All the best! |
| Das ändert alles. | That changes everything. |
| Alles verstanden? | Understood everything? / Got it? |
| Vor allem | Above all / Especially |
| Trotz allem | Despite everything |

**Example:** "Ich habe alles verstanden." - I understood everything.

---

### `niemand` (no one / nobody) [CORE]
**Rule:** Negates "jemand". No double negation needed in German - "niemand" alone is sufficient.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Das weiß niemand. | No one knows that. |
| Niemand hat reagiert. | Nobody reacted. |
| Das interessiert niemanden. | No one is interested in that. |
| Fast niemand | Hardly anyone |
| Niemand außer ihm | No one except him |

**Example:** "Das hat niemand gewusst." - No one knew that.

---

### `nichts` (nothing) [CORE]
**Rule:** Negates "etwas". Again, no double negation in German.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Das ändert nichts. | That changes nothing. |
| Nichts Besonderes. | Nothing special. |
| Gar nichts | Nothing at all |
| Nichts zu tun | Nothing to do |
| Das hat nichts damit zu tun. | That has nothing to do with it. |
| Nichts für ungut. | No offense. |
| Fast nichts | Almost nothing |

**Example:** "Das ändert nichts an der Tatsache, dass..." - That changes nothing about the fact that...

---

### `immer` (always) [CORE]
**Rule:** Indicates something happens every time without exception. Several key combinations change the meaning significantly.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Das ist immer so. | It's always like that. |
| Immer wieder | Again and again / Repeatedly |
| Fast immer | Almost always |
| Immer noch | Still (ongoing, up to now) |
| Immer mehr | More and more |
| Immer weniger | Less and less |
| Nicht immer | Not always |
| So gut wie immer | Just about always |

**Example:** "Er kommt immer zu spät." - He always comes late.

---

### `nie` / `niemals` (never) [CORE]
**Rule:** "nie" is everyday; "niemals" is more emphatic and absolute. "nie wieder" = "never again" is very common.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Das mache ich nie. | I'll never do that. |
| Nie wieder. | Never again. |
| Das habe ich nie gesagt. | I never said that. |
| Fast nie | Almost never / Hardly ever |
| Das war noch nie so. | It was never like that before. |
| Ich werde das niemals vergessen. | I will never forget that. |

**Example:** "Das habe ich nie gesagt." - I never said that.

---

### `immer noch` (still) / `nicht mehr` (no longer) [CORE]
**Rule:** These two are a pair. "Immer noch" = something continues. "Nicht mehr" = something has stopped.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Ich lerne immer noch Deutsch. | I'm still learning German. |
| Bist du immer noch hier? | Are you still here? |
| Er arbeitet nicht mehr dort. | He no longer works there. |
| Das gibt es nicht mehr. | That doesn't exist anymore. |
| Ich bin immer noch nicht fertig. | I'm still not done. |

**Example:** "Bist du immer noch nicht fertig?" - You're still not done?

---

### `überall` (everywhere) [EXTENDED]

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Das sieht man überall. | You see that everywhere. |
| Überall in Deutschland | Everywhere in Germany |
| Das gibt es überall. | You can find that everywhere. |
| Überall dabei sein | To be everywhere at once |

**Example:** "Das Thema ist gerade überall." - That topic is everywhere right now.

---

### `irgendwo` (somewhere) / `nirgends` (nowhere) [EXTENDED]

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Ich habe es irgendwo gesehen. | I saw it somewhere. |
| Das muss irgendwo sein. | It must be somewhere. |
| Das findet man nirgends. | You won't find that anywhere. |
| Nirgendwo anders | Nowhere else |

**Example:** "Ich muss das irgendwo hingelegt haben." - I must have put it somewhere.

---

### `jemand` (someone) / `irgendwer` (anyone) [CORE]

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Kann jemand helfen? | Can someone help? |
| Hat jemand mein Handy gesehen? | Has anyone seen my phone? |
| Das weiß doch jemand. | Surely someone knows that. |
| Irgendwer muss das wissen. | Someone must know that. |
| Gibt es jemanden, der...? | Is there someone who...? |

**Example:** "Hat jemand eine Frage?" - Does anyone have a question?

---

### `sowieso` / `ohnehin` (anyway / in any case) [CORE]
**Rule:** "sowieso" is more colloquial, "ohnehin" slightly more formal - both mean the same. Signal that the action/fact was already the case regardless.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Das hätte ich sowieso gemacht. | I would have done that anyway. |
| Das spielt sowieso keine Rolle. | That doesn't matter anyway. |
| Das weiß er ohnehin schon. | He already knows that anyway. |
| Das ist ohnehin klar. | That's obvious anyway. |
| Ich muss sowieso dort vorbei. | I have to go past there anyway. |

**Example:** "Das hätte ich sowieso gemacht." - I would have done that anyway.

---

### `zumindest` / `wenigstens` (at least) [CORE]
**Rule:** "wenigstens" is more colloquial and spoken; "zumindest" is slightly more formal and also common in writing. Both set a minimum acceptable outcome.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Das hätte er zumindest sagen können. | He could have at least said that. |
| Zumindest das ist klar. | At least that is clear. |
| Das ist wenigstens ehrlich. | That's at least honest. |
| Ich hoffe wenigstens, dass... | I at least hope that... |
| Zumindest im Prinzip. | At least in principle. |

**Example:** "Das hätte er zumindest erklären können." - He could have at least explained that.

---

### `kaum` (barely / hardly) [CORE]
**Rule:** Signals something is at the very edge of possibility or frequency. Often used for disbelief or something surprising.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Das ist kaum möglich. | That's barely possible. |
| Kaum zu glauben. | Hard to believe. |
| Ich kann kaum warten. | I can hardly wait. |
| Kaum jemand weiß das. | Hardly anyone knows that. |
| Das ist kaum der Rede wert. | That's hardly worth mentioning. |
| Ich habe kaum geschlafen. | I barely slept. |

**Example:** "Das ist kaum zu glauben." - That's hard to believe.

---

### `fast` (almost) [CORE]
**Rule:** Something nearly happened or nearly applies. Very common in everyday speech.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Fast fertig. | Almost done. |
| Fast alle | Almost everyone |
| Das ist fast dasselbe. | That's almost the same. |
| Ich hätte das fast vergessen. | I almost forgot that. |
| Fast jeden Tag | Almost every day |
| Das hat fast geklappt. | That almost worked. |

**Example:** "Ich hätte das fast vergessen!" - I almost forgot that!

---

### `gar` (not at all / whatsoever) [CORE]
**Rule:** Intensifies a negation. "gar nicht" = "not at all". Can also appear alone for emphasis. "gar nichts" = "absolutely nothing".

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Gar nichts | Nothing at all |
| Gar nicht | Not at all |
| Das ist gar nicht so einfach. | That's not so easy at all. |
| Das gefällt mir gar nicht. | I don't like that at all. |
| Das ist gar keine schlechte Idee. | That's actually not a bad idea. |
| Das stimmt gar nicht. | That's not true at all. |

**Example:** "Das gefällt mir gar nicht." - I don't like that at all.

---

### `überhaupt` (at all / in general / even) [CORE]
**Rule:** Three uses: (1) in negative sentences = "at all", (2) in questions = "even / actually", (3) generally = "in general". One of the most versatile words.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Überhaupt nicht | Not at all |
| Das stimmt überhaupt nicht. | That's not true at all. |
| Was meinst du überhaupt? | What do you even mean? |
| Hast du überhaupt zugehört? | Were you even listening? |
| Das gilt überhaupt für alle. | That applies to everyone in general. |
| Wer bist du überhaupt? | Who even are you? |
| Das interessiert mich überhaupt nicht. | I'm not interested in that at all. |

**Example:** "Hast du das überhaupt gelesen?" - Did you even read that?

---

### `erst` (only / not until / first) [EXTENDED]
**Rule:** Often misread as "first", but in time expressions it usually means "not until" or "only just". "erst dann" = "only then".

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Erst dann. | Only then. |
| Erst jetzt verstehe ich das. | Only now do I understand that. |
| Das habe ich erst heute erfahren. | I only found out today. |
| Er kommt erst morgen. | He's not coming until tomorrow. |
| Erst mal abwarten. | Let's wait and see first. |

**Example:** "Das habe ich erst gestern erfahren." - I only found that out yesterday.

---

### `noch` (still / yet / another / even more) [CORE]
**Rule:** Multiple uses - (1) "still" ongoing, (2) "not yet" with negation, (3) "another/more", (4) intensifies comparatives.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Noch nicht | Not yet |
| Noch einmal | Once more / Again |
| Noch mehr | Even more |
| Noch besser | Even better |
| Hast du noch...? | Do you have another...? |
| Das ist noch nicht fertig. | That's not done yet. |
| Ich brauche noch Zeit. | I still need time. |
| Noch eine Frage. | One more question. |

**Example:** "Das ist noch nicht fertig." - That's not done yet.

---

### `schon` as a time word (already / yet in questions) [CORE]
**Rule:** As a time marker (not the particle from Section 1), "schon" signals something happened earlier than expected.

| Common Phrases | English |
|---|---|
| Schon fertig? | Done already? |
| Das kenne ich schon. | I already know that. |
| Schon wieder | Again already / Once again |
| Hast du schon gegessen? | Have you already eaten? |
| Bist du schon da? | Are you already there? |
| Das haben wir schon besprochen. | We've already discussed that. |

**Example:** "Hast du das schon gehört?" - Have you already heard that?
